Future company

Vision

This is how I would set up a company of my own. Operating rules first. We will keep adding sections as the picture gets clearer.

Personal surface. Not indexed.

Calendar & meetings

No lunch meetings. Eat. Reset. Do not turn the break into another call.

No meetings after 4:00. Not much gets done late. People need time to wind down the day.

No first-thing-in-the-morning meetings. An 8:00 kickoff is not how we start. Give people time to get oriented before the calendar owns them.

Default to 15 minutes. Get away from the 30-minute habit. Aim for 15. Cap normal meetings at 30 minutes.

Two-hour meetings are not OK for regular work. Status meetings, syncs, standups, demos-as-status: none of that should run two hours. No exceptions for the usual meeting types.

IM before a meeting. If a quick message can handle it, use IM. A lot of calendar clutter is BS that should have been a short chat.

Email before you book time. Prefer email (or a written thread) before a meeting is set. Meetings are for what writing cannot settle cleanly, not the default first move.

Vacation

No tiered vacation cliffs. The usual model (two weeks until year five, then a third week at year ten) is out. Tenure should open time steadily, not in rare jumps.

Start with two weeks (10 days). Then every completed year of work earns you one extra vacation day for the years ahead. Think of it like restricted stock units, but for time off: each year of tenure unlocks another day into your annual bank.

Examples:

  • Year 0 (start): 10 days
  • After 1 year: 11 days
  • After 2 years: 12 days
  • After 19 years: 29 days
  • After 20 years: 30 days

Stay longer, earn more rest. The bump lands each year you complete, not in five-year cliffs.

Remote-first

Remote-first by default. The cheapest office is no office. We do not lease a permanent HQ unless we are genuinely huge and can afford one without pretending it is required for culture.

No strict hybrid quotas. If the work can be done remotely, we do not invent fake structure like “two days a week in office” or “three days a week or else.” That is theater.

Work from elsewhere is allowed. Want to work out of the country for a month? Fine, if the role can stay remote and the work still gets done. Trust outcomes, not badges on a door.

Shared meetup days, not attendance theater. People still need to meet people. We plan occasional shared days (monthly, or every couple of weeks) for team building, demos, and face time. Rent a WeWork conference room or similar for those days. Not a daily commute mandate. Strongly encouraged so the team stays human, not a punishment calendar.

See the company, see the world

International is a feature. If we grow into an international company with locations, people should be empowered to visit them, not stuck forever in one seat on a map.

Explore operations and cultures. Want to spend time at the Turkey location, or Mexico, or wherever we plant a flag? You should be able to go, work from that office, meet coworkers from different places, and learn how the company actually runs elsewhere.

That is career development and team building in one move: see the business, see the world, bring the perspective home.
